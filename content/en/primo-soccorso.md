> **In an emergency always call [112](tel:112).** These notes are a basic reminder and **do not replace** a first-aid course or the intervention of rescuers. Always follow the 112 operator's instructions.

Knowing **what to do in the first few minutes** can make the difference. Here are essential pointers for the most common emergencies. To gain real skills, take a [first-aid and BLSD course](/en/formazione/) with the Misericordia.

## Golden rules

1. **Stay calm** and make the scene safe for you and the casualty (traffic, electricity, gas).
2. **Call 112**: say where you are, what happened, how many people and their condition. Don't hang up until told to.
3. **Do not move** the person unless in immediate danger, especially after trauma.
4. **Stay with them** and keep talking until help arrives.

## Common emergencies

- **Cardiac arrest (no response, not breathing):** call 112, ask for a defibrillator (AED), start **chest compressions** in the centre of the chest, hard and fast (100–120/min). If trained, 30 compressions to 2 rescue breaths; if not, compressions only. Follow the AED's voice prompts.
- **Choking (cannot breathe, speak or cough):** give **5 back blows** between the shoulder blades, then **5 abdominal thrusts** (Heimlich); alternate until cleared. If they collapse, call 112 and start compressions.
- **Fainting:** lay the person down and **raise their legs**; loosen tight clothing; if they don't recover quickly, call 112.
- **Severe bleeding:** press firmly on the wound with a clean cloth; don't remove it if soaked — add another on top; call 112 for heavy bleeding.
- **Burn:** cool under **running cool water for 15–20 minutes**; don't apply ice, oil or creams; cover with a clean cloth; call 112 if extensive or deep.
- **Heatstroke:** move to shade/cool place, cool the body, give small sips of water if conscious; call 112 if confused or unconscious.
