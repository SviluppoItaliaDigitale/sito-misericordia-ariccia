The Misericordie are among the oldest volunteer associations in the world: a tradition of charity almost **eight centuries** old, in the sign of help to one's neighbour and of the *Works of Mercy*. The Misericordia di Ariccia, founded in 1994, is part of this great Movement.

## Origins

The Movement's roots lie in medieval **Florence**. In **1244** the Dominican friar Peter of Verona gave the Florence Misericordia its first constitutions; he was proclaimed a saint in 1253. The earliest known document mentioning the Misericordia dates to **1321**.

## The "buffa"

From the beginning the confratelli wore a service garment — a hood called the **"buffa"** that covered the face — so that all appeared equal, regardless of rank, and the helper stayed anonymous, following the Gospel principle *"do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing."*

## Spread and the Movement

Following the Florentine example, similar confraternities arose across Tuscany and then throughout Italy, and even abroad (the Portuguese *Santas Casas da Misericórdia* from 1498). On **24–25 September 1899**, gathered in Pistoia, the Misericordie founded their Confederation.

## The Misericordie today

Today the **National Confederation of the Misericordie of Italy** brings together over **800** confraternities, **670,000** members and **80,000** active volunteers. The Misericordia di Ariccia carries this ancient calling into the Castelli Romani: to stand beside those who suffer.
